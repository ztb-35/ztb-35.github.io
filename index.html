About

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Louisiana State University. My research focuses on leveraging large language models and statistical modeling to make time-series forecasting more accurate, interpretable, and robust in high-stakes applications such as transportation and autonomous driving.

I collaborate closely with researchers in AI, transportation, and biomechanics, working on projects ranging from LLM-driven forecasting frameworks to digital twins for athlete injury risk assessment.
