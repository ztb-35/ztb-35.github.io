About

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Louisiana State University. My research focuses on leveraging large language models and statistical modeling to make time-series forecasting more accurate, interpretable, and robust in high-stakes applications such as transportation and autonomous driving.

I collaborate closely with researchers in AI, transportation, and biomechanics, working on projects ranging from LLM-driven forecasting frameworks to digital twins for athlete injury risk assessment.

Research

Selected publications and preprints. (* indicates equal contribution.)

Enhancing LLM’s Interpretability for Time Series via Multi-Level Aligned Embeddings

ICLR 2025 (under review)

Proposes an interpretable LLM-based forecasting framework that decomposes signals into trend/seasonal components and aligns them with linguistic anchors to improve both accuracy and transparency.

Pruning and Malicious Injection: A Retraining-Free Backdoor Attack on Transformer Models

Security / Robustness (in preparation)

Introduces HPMI, a backdoor attack that prunes low-importance attention heads and injects pre-trained malicious heads, enabling efficient and stealthy attacks without full retraining.

PDF Code

Regularized Bayesian Factor Correlations in Confirmatory Factor Analysis

Psychometrics (in preparation)

Compares Lasso, Horseshoe, and SSP-based priors for estimating sparse factor correlation structures, highlighting trade-offs in false positives and interpretability.

Projects

Fault Injection for ADAS (OpenPilot)

Safety-critical AI · Ongoing

Designed a spatiotemporal fault-injection framework for ADAS that combines metric-guided bit search and context-aware timing to reveal safety-critical vulnerabilities in closed-loop driving simulations.

Athlete Digital Twin for ACL Injury Risk

Sports Analytics · Ongoing

Building a multimodal pipeline that fuses time-series performance data with movement videos to assess ACL injury risk and support rehab planning for soccer athletes.

LLM-Driven Time-Series Forecasting

Time Series · Completed

Developed multi-level alignment mechanisms between time-series segments and text prototypes, enabling LLMs to capture temporal patterns with interpretable anchors.
